A1 — Polar Tangent Slope (dy/dx at Top of Circle) · \(0\)
📄 Question

Find the slope of the tangent line to \(r = 3\sin\theta\) at \(\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}\).

(a) 0    (b) \(\sqrt{3}\)    (c) 2    (d) 3
📋 Formulas Needed
1
Polar slope \(\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{r'\sin\theta+r\cos\theta}{r'\cos\theta-r\sin\theta}\) Exam Sheet #7: "First Derivative (Parametric)"
📝 Full Solution
1
Find \(r'\) and evaluate at \(\theta=\pi/2\)

\(r'=3\cos\theta\). At \(\theta=\pi/2\): \(r=3\), \(r'=0\).

r' = 0 at the top of the circle (horizontal tangent).

2
Apply formula

\[\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{0\cdot 1+3\cdot 0}{0\cdot 0-3\cdot 1}=\frac{0}{-3}=\boxed{0}\]

sin(π/2)=1, cos(π/2)=0. Numerator = 0, so slope = 0 (horizontal tangent).

✅ Answer: (a) \(0\)
⚡ Quick Guide — Polar Tangent Slope
1Compute \(r'(\theta)\)
2Evaluate \(r\) and \(r'\) at the given \(\theta\)
3Plug into \(\frac{r'\sin\theta+r\cos\theta}{r'\cos\theta-r\sin\theta}\)
4If numerator = 0 → horizontal. If denominator = 0 → vertical.

A7 — Critical Points (Set Partials to Zero) · \((4,2)\) and \((0,0)\)
📄 Question

Find all critical points of \(h(x,y)=y^2-2y-y\sqrt{x}+\frac{x}{2}\) where \(x\ge 0\).

(a) \((4,2),(0,0)\)    (b) \((0,0)\) only    (c) \((2,4),(0,0)\)    (d) \((4,2)\)
📋 Formulas Needed
1
Critical point condition \(\nabla h=\mathbf{0}\) NOT on exam sheet → Ch 7 Formula Sheet: "Critical Point Condition"
📝 Full Solution
1
Compute partials

\(h_x = -\frac{y}{2\sqrt{x}}+\frac{1}{2}\), \(h_y = 2y-2-\sqrt{x}\).

Differentiate with respect to each variable.

2
From \(h_x=0\): \(\frac{y}{2\sqrt{x}}=\frac{1}{2}\) → \(y=\sqrt{x}\)

Sub into \(h_y=0\): \(2\sqrt{x}-2-\sqrt{x}=0 \Rightarrow \sqrt{x}=2 \Rightarrow x=4\), \(y=2\).

Also check \(x=0\): \(h_y(0,y)=2y-2=0 \Rightarrow y=1\). But checking: at \(x=0\), \(h_x\) involves \(1/\sqrt{x}\) which is undefined. The point \((0,0)\) is a boundary critical point where the gradient doesn't exist.

Solve the system. Don't forget boundary cases where partials are undefined.

✅ Answer: (a) \((4,2)\) and \((0,0)\)
⚡ Quick Guide — Finding Critical Points
1Set \(f_x=0\) and \(f_y=0\)
2Solve the system simultaneously
3Check boundary/corner points where partials are undefined

B1 [8 marks] — Lagrange Multipliers (Linear f on Ellipsoid) · Max = 2, Min = −2
📄 Question

Using Lagrange multipliers, determine the local extrema of \(f(x,y,z)=x+2y-2z\) subject to \(x^2+2y^2+4z^2=1\).

📋 Formulas Needed
1
Lagrange multipliers \(\nabla f=\lambda\nabla g\) NOT on exam sheet → Ch 7 Formula Sheet: "Lagrange Multiplier Method"
📝 Full Solution
1
Lagrange equations

\(\nabla f=(1,2,-2)\), \(\nabla g=(2x,4y,8z)\).

\(1=2\lambda x\), \(2=4\lambda y\), \(-2=8\lambda z\).

Each component gives one equation.

2
Solve for x, y, z in terms of λ

\(x=\frac{1}{2\lambda}\), \(y=\frac{1}{2\lambda}\), \(z=\frac{-1}{4\lambda}\).

Isolate each variable from its equation.

3
Apply constraint

\(\frac{1}{4\lambda^2}+\frac{2}{4\lambda^2}+\frac{4}{16\lambda^2}=1 \Rightarrow \frac{1}{4\lambda^2}+\frac{1}{2\lambda^2}+\frac{1}{4\lambda^2}=1 \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\lambda^2}=1 \Rightarrow \lambda=\pm 1\).

Substitute x, y, z into the constraint and solve for λ.

4
Find critical points and values

\(\lambda=1\): \((\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2},-\frac{1}{4})\), \(f=\frac{1}{2}+1+\frac{1}{2}=\boxed{2}\) (max).

\(\lambda=-1\): \((-\frac{1}{2},-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{4})\), \(f=-\frac{1}{2}-1-\frac{1}{2}=\boxed{-2}\) (min).

Two values of λ give two critical points. Compare f-values to classify.

✅ Answer: Max = 2 at \((\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2},-\frac{1}{4})\). Min = −2 at \((-\frac{1}{2},-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{4})\).
⚡ Quick Guide — Lagrange (Linear f)
1Set up \(\nabla f=\lambda\nabla g\)
2Solve each variable in terms of λ
3Substitute into constraint → solve for λ
4Back-substitute → compare f-values
